Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about diabetes management and MyDigiDia Record.

How do I add a glucose reading?
Login → Go to Records → Click "Add Record" → Enter glucose level, date, and reading type → Click Save. Your reading appears in analytics immediately.
What is a normal blood glucose level?
Fasting: 70–100 mg/dL (normal), 100–125 (pre-diabetic), 126+ (diabetic). Post-meal (2 hrs): below 140 mg/dL is normal. Always confirm targets with your doctor.
Can I delete or edit my records?
Yes. Each record on the Records page has Edit and Delete buttons. Deletions are permanent and cannot be undone, so please confirm before deleting.
What foods should I avoid with high blood sugar?
Avoid sugary drinks, white bread, white rice, fried foods, and processed snacks. Prefer whole grains, legumes, and non-starchy vegetables for stable glucose levels.
How often should I check my glucose?
Type 1: 4–10 times daily. Type 2 on insulin: 2–4 times daily. Type 2 on oral meds: fasting and 2-hrs post-meal as advised by your doctor.
What exercise is best for diabetes?
Walking 30 min/day, yoga, cycling, and light resistance training are excellent. Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity per week. Check glucose before and after exercise.
Can stress affect my blood sugar?
Yes. Stress releases cortisol which raises glucose. Meditation, deep breathing, regular sleep, and gentle exercise significantly help in long-term glucose control.
How do I import data from mySugr?
Export from mySugr (Settings → Export Data → CSV). In MyDigiDia, go to Records → Import CSV → Select the file → Map columns → Click Import. Duplicates are auto-skipped.
Should I drink more water?
Absolutely. 8–10 glasses/day helps kidneys flush excess glucose, reduces dehydration from high blood sugar, and supports overall metabolic health.
Is my medical data safe and private?
Yes. Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text. Your records are accessible only through your authenticated account. Always log out on shared devices.
Can I skip meals if my sugar is high?
No — skipping meals destabilizes glucose and may trigger hypoglycemia if you're on medication. Always follow a balanced meal schedule and consult your doctor.

Quick Diabetes Tips

Daily reminders to keep your blood sugar in a healthy range.

Stay Hydrated

Drink 8–10 glasses of water daily. Hydration helps kidneys remove excess glucose and reduces fatigue.

Eat Smart

Choose high-fiber, low-GI foods. Fill half your plate with non-starchy vegetables at every meal.

Move Daily

Walk 30 minutes every day. A post-meal 10-minute walk can lower blood sugar by 15–20%.

Monitor Regularly

Log readings consistently at the same time each day. Patterns help your doctor fine-tune treatment.

Know Warning Signs

Shakiness, sweating = low sugar. Extreme thirst, blurry vision = high sugar. Act immediately.

How to Add Records

Follow these steps to log your daily blood glucose readings in MyDigiDia Record.

1
Log in to your account using your email and password.
2
Navigate to the Records page from the top navigation bar.
3
Click "Add Record" and enter your glucose level in mg/dL.
4
Select the reading type: Fasting, Post-meal, Bedtime, or Random.
5
Add optional notes (meal eaten, exercise) and click Save.

Tip: Record at consistent times each day for more accurate trend analysis.

Import CSV Data

Transfer your glucose history from mySugr or any CSV-compatible diabetes app into MyDigiDia.

1
Export data from mySugr: Settings → Export Data → CSV format.
2
In MyDigiDia, go to Records → Import CSV at the top of the page.
3
Select your exported CSV file from your device.
4
Review the column mapping — match date, time, and glucose columns correctly.
5
Click Import. Duplicates are automatically skipped.

Supported: mySugr CSV and generic glucose CSV with date/time/value columns.

Reports & Analytics

MyDigiDia provides powerful analytics to help you and your doctor understand your diabetes patterns.

  • Glucose Trend Chart: Daily glucose with low/normal/high color coding.
  • Time in Range: % of readings within your target glucose range.
  • HbA1c Estimate: 3-month average converted to HbA1c percentage.
  • Average by Period: Morning, afternoon, evening, and bedtime breakdowns.
  • Highest / Lowest: Identify extreme readings and when they occurred.
  • Export PDF: Download a full report to share with your healthcare provider.

Filter by date range in Reports for focused insights before doctor visits.

Medication Reminder Guide

Stay on top of your medication schedule with reminders configured in your Settings.

1
Go to Settings → Medication Reminders section.
2
Add your medication name, dosage, and schedule (daily, twice daily, etc.).
3
Enable browser notifications when prompted so alerts appear in the background.
4
You'll receive a notification at the set time each day reminding you to take medication.

Always follow your doctor's prescription. Never adjust dosage without medical advice.

Account & Security

Your medical data is protected with industry-standard security practices.

  • Encrypted Passwords: Passwords are hashed — never stored in plain text.
  • Session Security: Auto-logout after inactivity protects shared device access.
  • OTP Reset: Password resets require a one-time code sent to your email.
  • Private Data: Records accessible only through your authenticated account.

Security Tips:

  • Use a strong, unique password with letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Never share your login credentials with anyone.
  • Always log out after using a shared computer.
  • Update your password regularly in Settings → Change Password.

Diet & Nutrition Guide

Food plays the most direct role in blood glucose management.

Foods That Help:

  • Leafy greens (spinach, fenugreek, kale)
  • Whole grains (oats, brown rice, whole wheat)
  • Legumes (lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans)
  • Low-GI fruits (apples, berries, pears, guava)
  • Nuts and seeds (almonds, walnuts, flaxseeds)

Foods to Avoid:

  • Sugary drinks (sodas, packaged juices, energy drinks)
  • White bread, white rice, refined flour products
  • Fried and ultra-processed snacks
  • Sweets, desserts, and candy

Use MyDigiDia's Diet Tracker to log meals and monitor their glucose impact.

Emergency Diabetes Tips

Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia) — Below 70 mg/dL

Symptoms: Shakiness, sweating, confusion, rapid heartbeat, dizziness, pale skin.

Action: Take 15–20g fast-acting carbs (4 glucose tablets, half cup juice, or 3 tsp sugar). Re-check after 15 minutes. Repeat if still low.

High Blood Sugar (Hyperglycemia) — Above 250 mg/dL

Symptoms: Extreme thirst, frequent urination, blurry vision, fatigue, fruity breath.

Action: Drink water, check for ketones (Type 1), contact your doctor. Do not exercise if above 300 mg/dL.

For any severe emergency, call 108 (India) or your local emergency services immediately.
