Daily Patient Diet Chart

Simple food choices for steady energy throughout the day.

Breakfast

7:00 AM - 9:00 AM
  • Oats, dalia, idli, poha with vegetables, or whole wheat toast.
  • Add egg, sprouts, dal, curd, paneer, or tofu.
  • Choose unsweetened tea, milk, or green tea.

Lunch

12:30 PM - 2:30 PM
  • 2 chapati or a small serving of rice with dal or curd.
  • Vegetable curry with salad on half the plate.
  • Include beans, fish, lean chicken, paneer, or tofu.

Snacks

4:00 PM - 6:00 PM
  • Apple, guava, orange, or papaya portion.
  • Roasted chana, nuts, sprouts, or buttermilk.
  • Avoid biscuits, sweets, and packaged snacks.

Dinner

7:30 PM - 9:00 PM
  • Keep dinner lighter than lunch.
  • Vegetable soup, dal, chapati, paneer, tofu, or lean protein.
  • Limit rice, fried foods, and late heavy meals.

Prefer More Often

  • Leafy vegetables, beans, gourds, cauliflower, cucumber, tomato, and salad.
  • Dal, chana, rajma, sprouts, curd, paneer, tofu, egg, fish, or lean chicken.
  • Whole grains in measured portions: chapati, oats, millets, dalia, or brown rice.

Limit Or Avoid

  • Sugar, sweets, sweet tea, desserts, sweetened juices, and soft drinks.
  • Bakery foods, fried snacks, chips, noodles, maida, and packaged snacks.
  • Oversized rice portions, very late dinners, and long gaps without food.

Plate Method

Fill half the plate with vegetables, one quarter with protein, and one quarter with controlled carbohydrates.

Meal Timing

Eat at regular times and avoid skipping meals, especially if your medicines can lower glucose.

After-Meal Walk

A short gentle walk after meals may help glucose control when your doctor says activity is suitable.

This diet guidance is educational and should not replace advice from your doctor or dietitian. Seek medical help for repeated low sugar, very high readings, dizziness, vomiting, chest pain, or severe weakness.
